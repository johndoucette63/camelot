## Overview

Originally scoped as US-2 of F5.1 (`014-indexers-quality`). Pulled out and deferred to a future-enhancements epic when an architectural conflict surfaced: **most paid private trackers (IPTorrents, TorrentLeech, BTN, HDBits, etc.) require a sustained ≥1:1 seed ratio**, which is incompatible with the current Deluge configuration (`stop_seed_ratio=0.0`, `remove_seed_at_ratio=true` — torrents are deleted immediately on completion).

Hit-and-run on a paid tracker leads to throttling and eventual ban, so US-2 cannot proceed without first resolving how to satisfy ratio without compromising the no-seed default.

## The decision blocker

| Constraint | Source |
|---|---|
| IPTorrents requires 1:1 ratio (and is the default candidate per F5.1 research R1) | https://www.ipoasis.com/blog/iptorrents-things-you-should-know |
| User's no-seed policy is intentional (storage, simplicity, privacy preference) | F5.1 user feedback during US-3 (2026-04-14) |
| TorrentLeech, BTN, HDBits also enforce ratio | research R1 |
| Donation-only music trackers (RED, OPS) waive ratio but are out of scope (no music need) | research R1 |

## Option matrix (snapshot — re-evaluate at decision time)

| Option | Trade-off | Cost |
|---|---|---|
| **A. Drop paid trackers, use public/semi-public** (1337x, TPB via Cardigann) | Less curated, more CloudFlare friction, lower seeders on niche content | $0 |
| **B. Hybrid Deluge label** — `iptorrents` label keeps seeding, default no-seed for everything else | Preserves default behavior; disk fills with ratio-active torrents until they hit 1:1 (days/weeks) | $0 + disk usage |
| **C. Seedbox front-end** — VPS does the seeding, local Deluge fetches via SFTP | Real cost, extra moving part, but cleanest separation | $5–10/mo |
| **D. Different paid tracker** — none of the major reputable private trackers waive ratio for video content | Same problem under a different name | varies |
| **E. Skip paid indexers entirely** — measure F5.1 US-1 (quality profiles) over 30+ days; revisit only if seeders/hit-rate prove inadequate | Defers the question; may discover paid indexers aren't needed | $0 |

**Tentative lean**: Option E. Run with the tightened quality profiles + existing free indexers, measure search hit-rate at T+30d (per F5.1 SC-002/SC-003/SC-007), and only revisit if the data shows a real gap. If it does, the data also tells us *what gap* (seeders? catalog breadth? CloudFlare bypass?), which informs whether B or C is the right answer.

## Out of scope

- Actually creating the account (paid signup is human-loop)
- Writing Prowlarr config for a chosen tracker (deferred until choice is made)
- Updating `docs/indexer-evaluation.md` (deferred — not yet authored)

## Re-entry triggers

Pull this back into an active feature when **any** of:

- T+30d F5.1 follow-up shows search hit-rate < 90% (SC-003) or seeder-count regressions
- A specific recurring grab-failure pattern emerges (e.g., obscure-show-only-on-private-trackers)
- Free indexers degrade materially (FlareSolverr breakage, CloudFlare-block escalation, etc.)
